How OpenAI Processes the Data

The system does not send the whole church database to OpenAI. The backend first prepares summarized budget data, then sends only the values needed to generate an allocation recommendation.

  • Unrestricted cash available
  • Projected unrestricted bills
  • Safe-to-spend amount
  • Ministry budget baseline and minimum requirement
  • Maximum cap per ministry
  • Priority score per ministry
  • Historical or current ministry spending reference

The backend computes the available unrestricted funds, subtracts projected unrestricted bills, determines the safe-to-spend amount, and compares this with ministry budget needs. Spending is adjusted before optimization so that emergency spikes, annual events, and seasonal expenses do not distort the recommendation as simple averages.

OpenAI generates the recommendation based on the available fund pool, ministry minimum requirements, maximum caps, priority scores, spending behavior, and the instruction that allocations must not exceed the available funds. Higher-priority ministries with valid needs may receive more, but the backend still validates and repairs the final output.

  • Do not allocate negative amounts.
  • Do not exceed the total funds available for optimization.
  • Respect each ministry's maximum cap.
  • Use each ministry only once.
  • Place any remaining amount into reserve or unallocated balance.
  • Allow authorized users to review and adjust before final approval.
